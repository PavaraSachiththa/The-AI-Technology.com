12 Most Advanced AI Humanoid Robots Shaping the World

  • Introduction

    • It’s almost about to be 2024 and if you aren’t aware of AI humanoid robots, then you’re missing out on a lot. Television series and even movies have given the premise of reality in which humans like robots will eventually dominate human beings. But in real life, the meaning of robots is exactly the opposite. In the real world, AI robots are designed to function like people in completing their duties.

    • Today, it can be confidently stated that Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning are the future of the current world. Robots are fascinating, and humans are using make things easier for them to manage. Moreover, robots are steadily entering our lives, and their manufacturing is increasing even more.

    • In this blog, you will learn about 12 realistic AI humanoid robots that can complete human-like jobs in society. Apart from the already widely known robot Sophia, you will find 11 other inspiring best human like robots.

  • Evolution of AI humanoid Robots

    • Originally used for vehicle operations and reiteration, robotics have broadened in the area and is now being employed in healthcare, hotels, and as assistants. These advanced AI humanoid robots are not only working smarter but also increasing and optimizing robots’ interface with human beings to keep up with the dynamic settings.

    • One of the most interesting usages is robotics in education. Robots such as NAO and Pepper are achieving the ability to teach coding, STEM skills, and problem-solving technology in classrooms, which may make those complicated subjects easier for students to learn. This facilitates creativity, innovation and teamwork while being an educational tool for the upcoming generation for a demanding society.

    • With increasing advancement in technology, advanced humanoid robots are natural, that enables them to make incredible impact within various fields. From automating mundane tasks in everyday life to nurturing young aspiring geniuses to get the most out of robotic facilities, they are building the future of amplified human capability harnessing robotic systems.

  • List of AI Humanoid Robots in 2024

      1. Sophia

    • The very first AI humanoid robot to be on our list is Sophia. Sophia is a human female robot, developed by Hanson Robotics based in Hong Kong. She is an advanced humanoid robot who can see, sustain contact, follow faces, and identify individuals.

    • Sophia’s power was turned on in February 2016, a few months after she was introduced to the world. Soon after, she became the world’s first AI robot citizen. She was even featured on Jimmy Falon’s show. This AI humanoid female robot uses a natural language subsystem to have conversations. Her creators say she can be extremely helpful in education, healthcare, and customer service.

    • Although Sophia is considered the most “human-like” robot, she is still far from being an idea. But, her physical attributes and the ability to have human-like expressions do give us a glimpse of what the future might look like.

      2. Beomni 1.0

    • An AI humanoid robot (with striking similarities to Dr. Who), beomni 1.0 assists humans with a wide-ranging general array of things, like medical services and manufacturing tasks, agriculture, etc. Additionally, its AI enables it to learn from operators and improve over time, blending human expertise with robotic efficiency.

    • It was developed by a US-based firm ‘beyond imagination’ and is a human-like robot with a form depicting a head, shoulders, waist, arms, and hands with opposable thumbs. Beomni 1.0 is a highly dexterous, cloud-controlled robotic system equipped with artificial intelligence (AI) and telepresence capabilities, allowing users to remotely operate the AI robot with precision and adaptability.

      3. Ameca Robot

    • Debuting as ‘The Future Face of Robotics’, Ameca is the world’s most advanced AI humanoid robot representing the forefront of human-robotics technology. It is designed specifically as a platform for development into future robotics technologies. In fact, she is perceived as the perfect humanoid robot platform for human-robot interaction.

    • Ameca knows how to turn heads with her hyper-realistic facial expressions. Its modular design also allows developers to upgrade or customize its capabilities, ensuring adaptability for future applications. The robot exemplifies the potential of combining AI with human-like interaction, providing a glimpse into the future of human-robot relationships.

      4. Toyota T-HR3

    • In 2017, Toyota proclaimed its return to creating the newest AI humanoid robot with its T-HR3. During that time, Toyota’s partner robot division had focused less on humanoid robots and more on other robotic ventures. Its HSR or Human Support Robot was a robot platform with a user-friendly mobile manipulator design. These friendly and helpful robots were designed to assist in household chores and simple tasks. However, they are not human like robots.

    • Toyota returned to the humanoid game with its T-HR3 and since its feature, the bot has revealed new technologies. These include ever better mobility, precise positioning, and in a test run, wireless operation. Moreover, it can even make drinks like a bartender. The T-HR3 has been designed for domestic as well as industrial use. You can operate it through a designated base with wearable controls for the system’s master arms and master foot.

      5. Nadine

    • Nadine is an empathetic artificially intelligent robot that was created for imitating human interactions and emotions. Nadine was created by a team at Nanyang Technological University in Singapore and is designed with artificial intelligence. It also has detailed facial features, with voice interaction and recognition features that allow her to carry out conversation. Nadine can recognize faces and associate them with prior engagements.

    • This makes her suitable for being an attractive assistant in customer-oriented service, care assistance for the elderly, and artificial social companion models. With her concrete thinking in terms of emotions and consequent reactions, she seems to be the first pioneer of the development of warm robotic machines capable of becoming a part of human society.

      6. Geminoid DK

    • Geminoid DK is a sophisticated AI humanoid robot that is intended to mimic an actual human being, Professor Henrik Scharfe from Denmark. The robot is from the Geminoid family which has been designed by Hiroshi Ishiguro Laboratories in Japan. From skin likeness, sharp facial features, and physical gesture to subtle mannerisms, all are designed to make it the best humanoid robot.

    • Geminoid DK is a valuable research instrument for exploring human-cross-cultural interaction and blurs the concept of human likeness and identity. Often used in experiments and demonstrations, Geminoid DK showcases the possibility to utilize androids as agents for education, communication, and ultramodern research of man’s behavior.

      7. Junko Chihira

    • Known to be the ‘best humanoid robot’, Junko Chihira tops the list of most attractive humanoid human female robots. Created by Toshiba, she is a very realistic-looking android who works in a new tourist information center in Japan. Junko is a multi-talented smart robot. She is well-versed in Japanese, Chinese, and English.

    • She made her debut in Aqua City Odaiba, a shopping center on Tokyo’s waterfront, in November 2015. Previously, she greeted visitors as they walked in, however, she currently provides tourists with information on events occurring in the area. Junko is the successor to another Toshiba android, Aiko Chihira. Like her, Junko could respond with a scripted, pre-set speech in the past. She gained speech recognition capabilities in 2017 and responded to tourists’ questions promptly.

      8. Jia Jia

    • Jia Jia is one of the most advanced humanoid robots created by a team based at the University of Science and Technology of China, or USTC. With her strikingly realistic look, Jia Jia is designed to look like a young Chinese woman, with detailed facial expressions that mirror human behavior as well as fluid movements. Its creators have provided it with the advanced capabilities of AI that allow it to support a conversation, recognize faces, and come up with responses in culturally proper expressions and humor.

    • Often called “robot goddess,” Jia Jia shines so much for its lifelike beauty and engaging nature. She is an example of the progress in robotics that reaches for an interhuman-to-machine gap at all costs regarding matters like service to customers, exhibitions, education, and so forth.

      9. ATLAS Robot

    • Atlas, termed “the world’s most dynamic humanoid,” was created to assist humans in dangerous tasks such as search and rescue missions. It was developed by American robotics company Boston Dynamics. The humanoid robot was funded by the U.S. Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA).

    • Although introduced in 2013, Atlas’s actual capabilities came to light in 2017. It is 1.8-meter (6 ft) tal and is known to perform amazing backflips. In the past few years, it has undergone various changes including some updates. Atlas was also seen performing a floor routine (similar to gymnasts), in a video released on September 24, 2019.

      10. Erica Robot by IRL

    • Erica, another advanced humanoid robot that made her debut in 2015. She is an advanced android designed as a platform to study human-robot interaction. She not only understands natural language but has a synthesized human-like voice and can display a variety of facial expressions.

    • Erica actually stands for ‘Erato Intelligent Conversational Android’. In 2016, a portrait of her sketched by Maija Tammi, a Finnish photographer, secured 3rd place in the UK National Portrait Gallery Competition. Erica’s features include using natural dialogue and motion-generation capabilities. With her ultrarealistic human-like appearance, her facial expressions and gesture motions make her appear very human. In fact, while listening, Erica blinks and moves her head just like a human.

      11. Pepper by SoftBank

    • Introduced to the world in 2014, Pepper is a semi-humanoid robot by SoftBank Robotics and has been designed with the ability to read emotions. Pepper was showcased in SoftBank Mobile phone stores in Japan during the initial days of its debut. The humanoid robot’s ability to recognize emotion is based on the detection and analysis of facial expressions and voice tones. However, Pepper’s production was paused in June 2021 due to less demand.

    • Pepper has been specifically designed to interact with humans. Standing 120cm tall, the robot perceives his environment without any trouble and can participate in a conversation upon seeing a human. It has a touch screen on its chest which displays content to highlight messages and support speech. Moreover, its curvy design ensures danger-free use and a high level of acceptance by users.

      12. Kime Robot

    • Popular as an AI humanoid robot bartender, Kime is developed by Macco Robotics and based in Seville, Spain. The robot has been designed to serve food and beverages. In fact, it can also serve a glass of beer in just 23 seconds!

    • About 2 square meters tall, Kime has a head, arms, and a torso which it uses to pour drinks. Moreover, it can perfectly align the glass at perfectly right angles for properly pouring the beer. This advanced humanoid robot is fast to serve approximately 300 glasses per hour.

  • Conclusion

    • With further technological development, AI humanoid robots become ever more intuitive, ushering in transformative contributions in many industries and to society as a whole. It can go beyond simple day-to-day tasks, where such robots can tackle other challenging issues, including health and educational concerns, even the much-needed care of individuals, enhancing the quality of life of many.

    • Tesla’s humanoid robot, Optimus, exemplifies the future of this technology, with its focus on automating repetitive tasks and making robotics more accessible for everyday applications. In the years to come, AI humanoid robots are sure to be household and workplace staples as they fuel innovation while reshaping how society interacts with technology.
